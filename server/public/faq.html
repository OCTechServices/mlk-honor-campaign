Frequently Asked Questions

About the Service
What is the MLK Honor Campaign?

A civic correspondence service inspired by Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s commitment to nonviolent civic engagement. It enables individuals to send a respectful, pre-written message to a U.S. Senator encouraging fidelity to democratic norms, moral leadership, and the rule of law.

Is this legal?

Yes. Writing to your elected officials is a protected form of civic expression under the First Amendment. This service does not constitute lobbying, protest, or political advocacy. Messages are pre-written, values-based, nonviolent, and non-partisan. No political position or candidate is endorsed.

Is this partisan or politically targeted?

No. The campaign is not aligned with any political party, candidate, or ideology. Messages focus on democratic norms, the rule of law, and human dignity — principles that transcend party affiliation. Users may select any currently serving U.S. Senator regardless of party.

Why can't I write my own message?

Pre-written, fixed message templates are a core design decision, not a limitation. They ensure every letter delivered through this service is:

  • Nonviolent and non-threatening
  • Values-based and dignified in tone
  • Consistent with the spirit of Dr. King's legacy
  • Transparently reviewable by anyone before purchase

No user-written content is accepted or delivered.

Which senators can I send a message to?

All currently serving U.S. Senators are available for selection. The senator list is sourced from public records and updated as needed.

Payment & Donations
Is my payment a donation?

No. Your payment is a service fee for message preparation and physical delivery. It is not a charitable donation, political contribution, or PAC payment. You are purchasing a civic correspondence service.

What does the service fee cover?

The fee covers message preparation, envelope addressing, USPS Certified Mail postage and handling, delivery tracking coordination, and administrative overhead. For reference, USPS Certified Mail with postage typically costs $5–$7 on its own.

Can I get a refund?

Once a message has been queued for delivery, the service fee is non-refundable. This is because operational preparation begins immediately upon confirmed payment. If you believe there was a payment error, contact us via the email on your Stripe receipt within 24 hours of purchase.

What is the $300 donation cap?

The platform operator independently donates to a registered charity once campaign receipts reach $300. This is a personal commitment made by the operator — it is not funded by user payments. The cap exists to keep the initiative bounded, transparent, and accountable. Progress toward the cap is publicly visible on the Progress page.

Where does the charitable donation go?

The platform operator donates directly to a registered 501(c)(3) charity aligned with civil rights, civic education, or community justice. User payments do not fund this donation — it comes entirely from the operator.

Delivery
How is my message delivered?

Messages are printed and sent via USPS Certified Mail to the senator's official Washington, D.C. office — the standard address used for formal constituent correspondence. Certified Mail provides a delivery record and requires a signature upon receipt.

How long does delivery take?

Letters are typically prepared and mailed within 3–5 business days of confirmed payment. USPS Certified Mail to a Washington, D.C. address generally arrives within 2–5 additional business days. Total time from payment to delivery is typically 5–10 business days.

How do I know my letter was actually sent?

If you would like delivery confirmation or a tracking number, contact us at the email address listed on your Stripe receipt. A team member will follow up with your USPS tracking information. Campaign-level delivery progress (letters queued vs. sent) is also publicly visible on the Progress page.

What if the senator's office rejects or returns the letter?

Delivery is attempted in good faith via standard USPS Certified Mail. If a letter is returned undeliverable, we will make one additional delivery attempt. The service fee is non-refundable in cases of rejection or return by the recipient's office, as the cost of preparation and postage is incurred regardless of the recipient's acceptance.

Privacy & Data
What personal information do you collect?

Minimal. The platform stores only what is operationally necessary: the senator selected, the message template chosen, a campaign identifier, and a reference to your Stripe payment session. No name, email address, or personal identifying information is stored in our systems. Stripe handles all payment data under their own privacy policy.

Is my information shared or sold?

No. We do not sell, share, or monetize any user data. The platform collects the minimum data required to operate the service and nothing more.
